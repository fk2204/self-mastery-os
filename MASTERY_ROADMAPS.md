# MASTERY ROADMAPS
## Complete Level-by-Level Progression for All 12 Life Modules

---

## 1. MONEY & WEALTH MODULE

### Systems Framework
**Inputs (Daily/Weekly):**
- Study 30 min on leverage/investing
- Practice high-value skill development
- Network with wealth builders
- Review/optimize one income stream

**Leading Indicators:**
- Skills acquired per quarter
- Value created per hour
- Network quality score
- Knowledge application rate

**Lagging Indicators:**
- Total net worth
- Passive income ratio
- Hourly rate trajectory
- Time-to-wealth-goal

**Feedback Triggers:**
- If hourly rate flat for 6 months: Learn new high-value skill
- If passive income <10% after 2 years: Build/buy leverage asset
- If net worth declining: Emergency audit + spending freeze

---

### Level 1: Awareness (Timeline: 2-4 weeks)
**Description:** You understand that trading time for money has limits.

**Capabilities:**
- Track where every dollar goes
- Calculate your true hourly rate
- Identify the difference between income and wealth
- Recognize basic leverage opportunities

**Key Actions Required:**
- Complete 30-day spending audit
- Calculate total work hours (including commute, prep, recovery)
- Divide annual income by total hours for true rate
- List all current income sources and categorize (time-bound vs leveraged)
- Read Naval's "How to Get Rich" tweetstorm

**Milestone:** Know exactly where every dollar goes for 30 days AND calculate true hourly rate with gap analysis.

**Key Resources:**
- Naval Ravikant: Specific knowledge, leverage principles
- Ramit Sethi: "I Will Teach You To Be Rich" (Chapter 1: Optimize spending)
- Brandon Turner: 1% rule for quick property analysis

---

### Level 2: Foundation (Timeline: 1-2 months)
**Description:** Building basic wealth-creation knowledge and mental models.

**Capabilities:**
- Understand compound interest mechanics
- Identify your specific knowledge gaps
- Distinguish income from wealth clearly
- Basic understanding of asset classes

**Key Actions Required:**
- Create personal skill development plan (3 high-value skills to learn)
- Calculate compound growth scenarios (10 years at 7%, 10%, 15%)
- Identify your specific knowledge (what you know that others pay for)
- Read one foundational wealth book cover to cover
- Open investment account (even if not funding yet)

**Milestone:** Written skill development plan with timeline and first $500 saved/invested.

**Key Resources:**
- Warren Buffett: Compound interest concept, index fund philosophy
- Naval Ravikant: Specific knowledge framework
- Morgan Housel: "Psychology of Money" mental models

---

### Level 3: Skill Builder (Timeline: 3-6 months)
**Description:** Actively developing income-multiplying skills.

**Capabilities:**
- Learning one high-leverage skill (code, writing, sales, design)
- Understand market value of different skills
- Basic networking for opportunities
- Package knowledge into teachable format

**Key Actions Required:**
- Commit to 100 hours in one high-value skill
- Complete 3 practice projects showcasing skill
- Join community of people 2-3 levels ahead
- Create portfolio or proof of capability
- Reach out to 20 potential clients/partners

**Milestone:** Complete first paid project using new skill (even if discounted/small).

**Key Resources:**
- Cal Newport: Deep work for skill acquisition
- Alex Hormozi: $100M Offers (understanding value)
- Naval Ravikant: Accountability, specific knowledge, leverage triad

## 2. SALES & PERSUASION MODULE

### Systems Framework
**Inputs (Daily/Weekly):**
- 10 cold contacts daily
- 2 sales calls/meetings
- 1 hour objection practice/role play
- Study one sales framework/week
- Follow up on all open opportunities

**Leading Indicators:**
- Outreach volume (contacts/day)
- Response rate
- Meeting booking rate
- Objections handled successfully
- Pipeline velocity

**Lagging Indicators:**
- Close rate
- Average deal size
- Customer lifetime value
- Referral rate
- Revenue growth

**Feedback Triggers:**
- If response rate <5%: Rewrite outreach messaging
- If meeting-to-close <10%: Focus on qualification
- If deal size flat: Practice value creation and pricing
- If referrals <20% of leads: Improve customer experience

---

### Level 1: Awareness (Timeline: 1-2 weeks)
**Description:** You understand sales is a learnable skill, not innate talent.

**Capabilities:**
- Recognize that everyone sells (ideas, services, yourself)
- Accept rejection as data, not personal failure
- Understand basic objection categories (time, money, trust, need)
- Make first cold outreach attempts

**Key Actions Required:**
- Make 10 cold outreach attempts (email, call, DM)
- Get rejected at least 5 times
- Write down every objection you hear
- Watch 3 sales call recordings (YouTube, courses)
- Read "Rejection Proof" or similar mindset book

**Milestone:** Complete first 10 cold outreach attempts without giving up.

**Key Resources:**
- Jordan Belfort: Straight Line System introduction, tonality basics
- Jeb Blount: Fanatical Prospecting mindset (rejection is normal)
- Chris Voss: Never Split the Difference (tactical empathy intro)

---

### Level 2: Foundation (Timeline: 2-4 weeks)
**Description:** Building basic sales communication and opening skills.

**Capabilities:**
- Open conversations confidently
- Ask basic qualifying questions (budget, authority, need, timing)
- Handle simple objections without freezing
- Follow up systematically

**Key Actions Required:**
- Book first meeting from cold outreach
- Practice opening scripts 50+ times out loud
- Create list of 20 qualifying questions
- Set up basic CRM or tracking system
- Role play with friend/colleague 3+ times

**Milestone:** Book first meeting from cold outreach.

**Key Resources:**
- Jeb Blount: Opening techniques, phone confidence
- Grant Cardone: 10X Sales principles, persistence
- Aaron Ross: Predictable Revenue (SDR framework for meetings)

---

### Level 3: Active Learner (Timeline: 2-3 months)
**Description:** Consistently practicing sales fundamentals with structure.

**Capabilities:**
- Use structured sales framework (BANT, MEDDIC, Straight Line)
- Handle 5+ common objections confidently
- Follow up 5-7 times systematically
- Control conversation frame

**Key Actions Required:**
- Choose one sales framework and master it
- Script responses to 10 most common objections
- Practice mirroring, labeling, calibrated questions (Voss)
- Make 100+ outreach attempts
- Close first deal from cold outreach

**Milestone:** Close first deal from cold outreach (even if small).

**Key Resources:**
- Jordan Belfort: Straight Line System (full framework)
- Chris Voss: Never Split the Difference (mirroring, labeling, calibrated questions)
- Jill Konrath: SNAP Selling for complex sales

---

### Level 4: Competent Closer (Timeline: 4-6 months)
**Description:** Reliably closing deals with predictable process.

**Capabilities:**
- Navigate full sales cycle from prospecting to close
- Handle complex objections (budget, competition, timing)
- Create urgency ethically (scarcity, risk of inaction)
- Maintain 20%+ close rate on qualified leads

**Key Actions Required:**
- Close 10+ deals using consistent process
- Record and review 5+ sales calls
- Build objection handling library (scripts for 20+ objections)
- Track metrics: calls → meetings → closes
- Optimize weakest part of funnel

**Milestone:** Maintain 20%+ close rate on qualified opportunities for 2 months.

**Key Resources:**
- Jordan Belfort: Tonality mastery, certainty scale, trial closes
- Grant Cardone: Urgency creation, commitment securing
- Chris Voss: Tactical empathy for objection diffusion
- Jill Konrath: Value-based selling for complex deals

---

### Level 5: Skilled Persuader (Timeline: 6-12 months)

**Capabilities:**

**Key Actions Required:**

**Milestone:** Consistently hit or exceed sales targets for 3+ months.

**Key Resources:**

## 3. PERSONAL FINANCE MODULE

### Systems Framework
**Inputs (Daily/Weekly):**
- 5-minute daily finance check (accounts, spending)
- Weekly budget review
- Monthly net worth calculation
- Quarterly portfolio rebalancing
- Annual tax optimization review

**Leading Indicators:**
- Savings rate (% of income saved)
- Spending vs budget variance
- Debt paydown velocity
- Investment contribution consistency
- Emergency fund months coverage

**Lagging Indicators:**
- Net worth
- Credit score
- Debt-to-income ratio
- Investment returns
- Financial independence progress

**Feedback Triggers:**
- If savings rate <20%: Audit and cut expenses
- If debt increasing: Freeze new debt, increase payments
- If emergency fund <3 months: Pause investing, build cash
- If spending variance >10%: Review budget categories

---

### Level 1: Awareness (Timeline: 1-2 weeks)
**Description:** You know exactly where your money goes.

**Capabilities:**
- Track every dollar for 30 days
- Identify spending categories
- Know total monthly expenses
- Understand income vs expenses

**Key Actions Required:**
- Complete 30-day spending audit (track everything)
- Categorize all expenses (housing, food, transport, etc.)
- Calculate monthly burn rate
- Identify 3 biggest spending categories
- Open separate checking/savings accounts

**Milestone:** 30 days of complete expense tracking with category breakdown.

**Key Resources:**
- Ramit Sethi: Conscious spending plan
- Dave Ramsey: Budget basics and awareness
- Mr. Money Mustache: Expense tracking and awareness

---

### Level 2: Foundation (Timeline: 1 month)
**Description:** Basic budgeting and expense control in place.

**Capabilities:**
- Create and follow monthly budget
- Separate needs from wants
- Automate bill payments
- Save 5-10% of income consistently

**Key Actions Required:**
- Create first monthly budget (50/30/20 or zero-based)
- Automate all bill payments
- Set up automatic savings transfer (payday → savings)
- Cut 3 unnecessary recurring expenses
- Save first $500 in emergency fund

**Milestone:** Follow budget for 1 full month and save $500 emergency fund.

**Key Resources:**
- Ramit Sethi: Automation and conscious spending
- Dave Ramsey: Baby Steps (emergency fund, budget)
- YNAB (You Need A Budget): Zero-based budgeting

---

### Level 3: Debt Manager (Timeline: 2-4 months)
**Description:** Actively managing and reducing debt.

**Capabilities:**
- List all debts with interest rates
- Choose debt payoff strategy (avalanche or snowball)
- Pay minimum + extra on target debt
- Avoid new consumer debt

**Key Actions Required:**
- Create debt inventory (amount, rate, minimum payment)
- Choose payoff method (highest rate first or smallest first)
- Pay off first small debt or make 20%+ dent in largest
- Negotiate 1-2 interest rate reductions
- Build $1,000 emergency fund (before aggressive payoff)

**Milestone:** Pay off first debt completely OR reduce total debt by 20%.

**Key Resources:**
- Dave Ramsey: Debt snowball method
- Ramit Sethi: Debt negotiation scripts
- Mr. Money Mustache: Anti-debt philosophy

---

### Level 4: Consistent Saver (Timeline: 4-8 months)
**Description:** Saving 15-20%+ of income systematically.

**Capabilities:**
- Save 15-20% of income automatically
- Maintain 3-month emergency fund
- Understand basic investment accounts (401k, IRA, brokerage)
- Optimize tax-advantaged accounts

**Key Actions Required:**
- Increase savings rate to 15-20% of gross income
- Build emergency fund to 3 months expenses
- Max out employer 401k match if available
- Open Roth IRA and make first contribution
- Automate all savings and investments

**Milestone:** Save 15%+ of income for 3 consecutive months + 3-month emergency fund.

**Key Resources:**
- Ramit Sethi: Automation and savings ladders
- JL Collins: Simple Path to Wealth (investment basics)
- Mr. Money Mustache: High savings rates

---

### Level 5: Investor (Timeline: 8-18 months)

**Capabilities:**

## 4. DATING & SOCIAL MODULE

### Systems Framework
**Inputs (Daily/Weekly):**
- 3+ social interactions daily (in-person preferred)
- 1 new conversation with stranger per day
- Weekly social event or activity
- Daily charisma/body language practice
- Monthly review of relationship quality

**Leading Indicators:**
- Number of new connections per week
- Quality conversation rate
- Social invitations received
- Comfort in social situations
- Positive interaction ratio

**Lagging Indicators:**
- Social circle size and quality
- Dating options/opportunities
- Relationship depth
- Social confidence level
- Friendship quality score

**Feedback Triggers:**
- If no new connections in 2 weeks: Attend new event/group
- If conversation anxiety high: Practice smaller interactions
- If social invites declining: Initiate more yourself
- If relationship conflicts increasing: Study conflict resolution

---

### Level 1: Awareness (Timeline: 2-4 weeks)
**Description:** You recognize social skills are learnable, not fixed.

**Capabilities:**
- Understand basics of body language
- Recognize your current social comfort zones
- Accept rejection/awkwardness as learning data
- Make eye contact in conversations

**Key Actions Required:**
- Have 10 conversations with strangers (cashier, barista, gym, etc.)
- Study basic body language (open vs closed posture)
- Practice eye contact and smiling
- Identify your social anxiety triggers
- Read one book on social skills or charisma

**Milestone:** Initiate 10 conversations with strangers without avoidance.

**Key Resources:**
- Dale Carnegie: How to Win Friends and Influence People (basics)
- Vanessa Van Edwards: Charisma on Command (body language)
- Mark Manson: Models (honest attraction principles)

---

### Level 2: Foundation (Timeline: 1-2 months)
**Description:** Building basic social confidence and conversation skills.

**Capabilities:**
- Start conversations with strangers comfortably
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen actively without planning your response
- Read basic social cues

**Key Actions Required:**
- Start 30+ conversations with new people
- Practice active listening (repeat back what you heard)
- Attend 4+ social events (meetups, classes, gatherings)
- Develop 3 conversation starters that feel natural
- Exchange contact info with 5+ new people

**Milestone:** Start and maintain conversation with stranger for 5+ minutes naturally.

**Key Resources:**
- Dale Carnegie: Genuine interest in others, listening skills
- Patrick King: Better Small Talk (conversation frameworks)
- Mark Manson: Vulnerability and honesty in connection

---

### Level 3: Active Learner (Timeline: 2-4 months)
**Description:** Consistently practicing social skills and building connections.

**Capabilities:**
- Make friends in new environments
- Tell engaging stories
- Handle awkward silences
- Transition conversations naturally

**Key Actions Required:**
- Join 1-2 regular social activities (sports league, class, hobby group)
- Develop 5 go-to stories (funny, meaningful, relatable)
- Practice storytelling structure (setup, tension, resolution)
- Organize 2+ small gatherings (dinners, outings)
- Follow up with new connections (text, call, invite to things)

**Milestone:** Make 3+ new friends through proactive social engagement.
